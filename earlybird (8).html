Key FindingsMixed Outlook

Middle East conflict and energy shock

High Risk

A U.S.-Iran ceasefire remains fragile with ongoing negotiations. The Strait of Hormuz blockade is disrupting global energy supply; the IMF has warned all economies will feel the impact as prices surge. Europe faces potential jet fuel shortages, threatening aviation and supply chains. Energy cost pressure feeds directly into inflation, rates, consumer spending and corporate margins.

Equity markets at record highs despite chaos

Bullish Signal

S&P 500 and Nasdaq are both printing all-time highs. Over 80% of reporting companies beat earnings expectations this cycle. Markets are pricing in conflict resolution and forward-looking on de-escalation. The risk: if the geopolitical bet is wrong, the correction could be sharp and swift.

Fed independence under political pressure

Watch Closely

The White House is escalating attacks on Fed Chair Jerome Powell, pushing for rate cuts and openly backing a replacement candidate. Any perceived erosion of central bank independence carries significant implications for rates, bond markets and USD positioning globally.

Corporate divergence: large caps vs. cost-sensitive names

Earnings

PepsiCo beat expectations on strong global sales. Netflix earnings are in focus following strategic repositioning. Spirit Airlines is at risk of liquidation due to surging fuel costs, illustrating the bifurcation between companies with pricing power and those exposed to input cost volatility.
